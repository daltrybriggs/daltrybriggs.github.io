Ceremony

Quaker weddings take the same simple, quiet form as the usual meetings for worship.

A Quaker marriage takes place during a specially-arranged meeting for worship. The couple becomes a partnership in a lifelong commitment to faithfulness and love. Each makes the same promise.

Who attends?

Members of the local meeting attend along with those who have been specially invited for the wedding. Everyone is asked to support the couple, silently or in spoken ministry.

Marriage declarations

At the start of the meeting a Quaker will give a brief overview of a Quaker wedding.

Early in the meeting, the couple hold each other's hands and make a solemn declaration of marriage. Each in turn uses these words: "Friends, I take this my friend, [name], to be my partner in marriage, promising, through divine assistance, to be unto him/her/[commonly used name] a loving and faithful partner in marriage, so long as we both on earth shall live."

After this exchange the Quaker marriage certificate is signed by the couple and we invite all who witnessed the wedding to sign our certificate. The local Registering Officer of the Society of Friends reads the certificate out loud, either immediately after the declarations have been made or towards the close of the meeting.

Wedding rings

Wedding rings play no formal part in Quaker marriages, but many couples like to give each other rings after they have made their declarations - and we will be doing this.

Speaking

The meeting continues as it began with a period of silence, during which anyone may become moved to speak, you can then stand and speak to the meeting, allowing an air of gathered stillness to re-emerge between each speaker. This can be a time when the couple is given inspiration and help that will be a source of strength to them during their married life.

It is also an opportunity for everyone there to ask God's blessing on the marriage or commit themselves to supporting the couple however they can.

The meeting closes after the elders have shaken hands. As soon as the meeting has ended, everyone is invited to sign the Quaker marriage certificate. While this is happening, the couple and their witnesses go with the Registering Officer to sign the civil marriage paperwork.
